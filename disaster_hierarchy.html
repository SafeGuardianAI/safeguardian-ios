True Peer-to-Peer Mesh Model

  • Nova Peer-to-Peer (P2P): Nova cells (1-5) are directly interconnected. They act as mesh relays, passing messages for each other. If User A wants to message User C, Nova 1 might relay through Nova 2.
  • Cross-Region Bridging: Even without a direct Trek connection, Nova agents can form "Ad-hoc Bridges" (e.g., Nova 3 to Nova 4) to link geographic regions Beta and Alpha.
  • Trek Tactical Backbone: Trek nodes (A and B) maintain their own high-priority mesh relay. They act as the "backbone" for the WorldGraph, ensuring that strategic data from Apex reaches the furthest edges of the network.
  • Emergent Integraph: The WorldGraph is not a central database; it is the *emergent result* of all this P2P Gossip. Every time a Nova relays a packet for another, it contributes to the collective understanding of the Mesh Topology and Vitals.
